What is a Master’s Degree?

A master’s degree is what you pursue once you have already finished advanced studies in your field. Basically, opting for a Master’s degree means that you wish to specialise in a specific area of your study. The master’s degree or postgraduate course may be 1 or 2 years long. Some of these courses cost relatively less than the under-graduate courses, except a few like Masters in Business Administration (MBA). Pursuing a Master’s degree involves undergoing in-depth study in your field such that it enables you to demonstrate a high level of expertise in your area of professional practice. Once you are a postgraduate, you possess a high level of knowledge and advanced skills and techniques associated with your area of study. This is why most companies today look to recruit postgraduate students.

Let’s look at the list of Master's degrees programs offered in India:

  • MBA
  • Master Of Human Resource Management [MHRM]
  • Post Graduate Diploma In Management [PGDM]
  • Master In Management Studies [M.M.S]
  • Master Of Personnel Management [MPM]
  • Master In Marketing Management [MMM]
  • Master In International Business [MIB]
  • Master in Commerce (M.Com)
  • Master Of Computer Applications(MCA)
  • Master Of Pharmacy [M.Pharm]
  • Master of Science (M.Sc.)
  • Master of Arts
